Pricing and Information

Traditional Media Pricing

Traditional Media covers all the media made by hand. This includes paintings of watercolor and acrylic, pencil drawings whether colored or simple line work. Anything make with chalk, or pastels. If more then 20% of your proposed project cannot be done on a computer consider it traditional media. This is more expensive for me to create versus digital art as everything must be done by hand on top of the fact that I need to source the materials for the art. Canvas, pencils, paint, etc., your commission will not just include my time like other mediums but also has to cover my costs of materials. If you are interested in a particular style but the prices here are a bit out of your range consider if you idea can be converted well into digital medium where software allows me to be more productive and materials are not added onto the prices.


Pieces start for a 8" x 8" piece of medium with one subject in black and white, with a base price of $100


Prices increase are at the artists discresion however generally speaking price increases are as follows:

- coloring +$50-150 (depnding on the medium this may be required ex. watercolors)
- additional subject +$50 (per added subject)
- complex background +$50-$100 (depending on complexity)

Be aware that prices are subject to change due to the estimated amount of labor the commission will take.

Digital Media Pricing

Digital Media covers anything that is flat art that can be created entirely on a computer that is not animated nor needs to be viewed from multiple angles (see 3-D for these). Digital prices start cheaper then traditional mediums due to no inital investment in materials. However, since the piece can be used and replicated infinetly for the sake of fair use liscensing options for large scale use may be applied.

3-D Media Pricing

3-D media is anything that must be rendered from multiple angels or in a 2-D piece that is animated (I.E. moves through time). This form of art is the most expensive because it generally just takes the most time. Animation is merely drawing the same drawing several times with mild variations to create movement. 3-D animation often needs to be considered from multiple side and therefore is like constructing the same piece from multiple angles layered on top of each other. 3-D art is often functional as well and that rapidly can increase the labor required. 3-D work includes simple colors for free but complex texture work increases depending on the commission, for example something being 1080 is less work then creating something with 4k textures.
